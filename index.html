ABOUT THE 5th AII WORKSHOP

AII Workshop

Augmented Intelligence and Interaction (AII) Workshop aims to bring together experts
in many sub-fields of AI, Human Computer Interaction (HCI), and Robotics. In this workshop,
multidisciplinary experts can share their latest advance and pave the way toward
grounded applications mixing AI, HCI, and robotics.

Duration

March 24th - March 25th

Venue

桃園大溪笠復威斯汀度假酒店
The Westin Tashee Resort, Taoyuan

Registration Fee

FREE

Registration Period

From now until 02/25

OUR SPEAKERS

Keynote Speakers

楊明玄 (Ming-Hsuan Yang)

Professor, University of California, Merced

Ming-Hsuan Yang is a Professor in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science at University of California, Merced. Professor Yang served as a program co-chair of IEEE International Conference on Computer Vision (ICCV) in 2019, program co-chair of Asian Conference on Computer Vision (ACCV) in 2014, and general co-chair of ACCV 2016. His research interests include computer vision, pattern recognition, AI, robotics, and machine learning.

View Details

劉洺堉 (Ming-Yu Liu)

Distinguished Research Scientist and Manager, NVIDIA

Ming-Yu Liu is a Distinguished Research Scientist and Manager at NVIDIA Research. His research interests focus on generative image modeling. His goal is to enable human-like imagination capability in machines.

View Details

賴尚宏 (Shang-Hong Lai)

Principal Researcher, Microsoft AI R&D Center

Shang-Hong Lai has been a faculty member in the Department of Computer Science, National Tsing Hua University, since 1999. Since the summer of 2018, Dr. Lai has been on leave to join Microsoft as a principal researcher in the Microsoft AI R&D Center, Taiwan. Dr. Lai’s research interests are mainly focused on computer vision, image processing, and ML. He has authored more than 200 papers that have been published in refereed international journals and conferences in these areas. In addition, he has been awarded around 30 patents on his research on computer vision.

View Details

阮大成 (Da-Cheng Juan)

Researcher, Google AI

Da-Cheng received his Ph.D. from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and his Master’s from Machine Learning Department, both at Carnegie Mellon University. His research interests include machine learning and energy-efficient computing. Da-Cheng has published more than 15 papers in the related fields. Meanwhile, he also enjoys algorithmic programming and has won several awards in major programming contests. Currently, Da-Cheng is affiliated with Google Research, working on large-scale, semi-supervised learning and deep learning.

View Details

謝卓叡 (Cho-Jui Hsieh)

Assistant Professor, UCLA

Cho-Jui Hsieh's research interests are in developing new algorithms and optimization techniques for large-scale machine learning problems. Currently, he is working on developing new machine learning models as well as improving the model size, training speed, prediction speed, and robustness of popular (deep learning) models.

View Details

陳維超 (Wei-Chao Chen)

Wei-Chao Chen is Chief AI Advisor at Inventec Inc. and co-founder at Skywatch Innovation Inc., a leading provider of cloud-based video products and solutions. He is also an adjunct faculty member at National Taiwan University. His research interests include graphics hardware, computational photography, augmented reality, and user interfaces. Dr. Chen was a senior research scientist at the Nokia Research Center at Palo Alto, where he co-authored papers and patents and developed applications related to mobile computer vision and computational photography. Prior to working at Nokia, he was a 3D graphics architect at NVIDIA between 2002 and 2006, and was the shader architecture lead for several GPUs, including the Sony Playstation 3 RSX GPU. While at Intel Corporation during 2000–2001, he co-developed light field mapping technology and the open source light field project (OpenLF)
